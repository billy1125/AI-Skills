# Literature Review Language

Use this file when revising literature review paragraphs, synthesizing prior studies, or diagnosing whether a paragraph properly identifies a research gap.

## Core functions

A literature review paragraph usually performs one or more of these functions:

1. Establish the research area.
2. Summarize prior findings.
3. Compare strands of scholarship.
4. Identify limitations or gaps.
5. Position the present study.

## Introducing prior research

Preferred expressions:
- Previous studies have examined...
- A growing body of research has investigated...
- Prior research has shown that...
- Several studies have focused on...
- Existing scholarship has emphasized...

Examples:
- Previous studies have examined the relationship between feedback and student engagement.
- A growing body of research has investigated how institutional support shapes academic persistence.

## Reporting specific studies

Preferred expressions:
- Smith (2020) argues that...
- Chen and Wang (2021) found that...
- Lee (2019) demonstrates that...
- Garcia (2022) identifies...
- According to Patel (2023),...

Use "argues" for conceptual claims, "found" for empirical findings, and "demonstrates" only when the evidence is strong.

## Synthesizing studies

Preferred expressions:
- Taken together, these studies suggest that...
- Collectively, this literature indicates that...
- These findings converge on the view that...
- Despite differences in context, these studies share a common emphasis on...
- This body of work highlights...

Examples:
- Taken together, these studies suggest that feedback is not merely an instructional practice but also a mechanism of academic support.

## Comparing strands of research

Preferred expressions:
- One strand of research emphasizes...
- Another line of scholarship focuses on...
- Whereas some studies highlight..., others examine...
- This perspective differs from...
- In contrast to research on X, studies of Y suggest...

Examples:
- Whereas some studies emphasize individual motivation, others focus on institutional conditions that shape student engagement.

## Identifying research gaps

Preferred expressions:
- However, less attention has been paid to...
- Few studies have examined...
- The relationship between X and Y remains underexplored.
- Existing research has not fully addressed...
- It remains unclear whether...
- More research is needed to understand...

Examples:
- However, less attention has been paid to how feedback quality affects first-year students in non-Western institutional contexts.
- Although prior studies have examined student engagement, the role of advising structures remains underexplored.

## Positioning the present study

Preferred expressions:
- To address this gap, the present study examines...
- Building on this literature, this study investigates...
- The present study extends prior research by...
- This study contributes to the literature by...
- In response to this gap, this paper analyzes...

Examples:
- To address this gap, the present study examines how feedback quality is associated with student engagement among first-year university students.

## Diagnosis criteria for literature review paragraphs

Check whether the paragraph:
- synthesizes rather than lists studies
- explains how prior studies relate to each other
- identifies a specific gap
- connects the gap to the present study
- avoids exaggerated claims of novelty

Common problems:
- Annotated-bibliography style: one study after another without synthesis.
- Vague gap: saying "few studies" without specifying what is missing.
- Unsupported novelty: claiming no research exists without evidence.
- Weak positioning: failing to explain how the present study responds to the gap.
